Build Notes

Rebuilt Site & Go-Live Checklist

← Back to start

A working, mobile-first rebuild of the Moberly Motorsports Park website. This is the demo that backs the proposal. Below is how it was built, what to confirm with the owner before it goes live, and what is in this folder.

How to open it

Double-click START-HERE.html. Everything opens in your browser and works offline. The only parts that need internet are the live side-by-side demo, the Google map on the Directions page, and the headline font, and all of those degrade gracefully without it.

Every audit problem, fixed

Hidden pages are back and visible to search. Nothing is trapped in an image: the schedule, prices, and rules are real text and tables you can edit in seconds. One phone number everywhere. Business and event data added so Google can show the track and its race nights. A real homepage with a headline, the next race up top, and a Buy Tickets button. The stale 2023 content is refreshed to 2026, the gallery uses real race photos instead of the old Wix stock images, "Wiener" is spelled correctly throughout, and there is an email signup so the track can reach fans directly.

Tickets, registration, and standings

These run on the track's existing MyRacePass system (track ID 3133). The rebuild links out to them with prominent buttons rather than embedding them, so the demo stays fast and nothing breaks. Buy Tickets, Driver Registration, and View Live Standings all jump to the real MyRacePass pages.

Confirm these with the owner before go-live

  • Phone number. The old site showed two: 660-833-3639 in the header and 660-651-8599 in the footer. The rebuild uses 660-833-3639 everywhere. Confirm that is the correct one.
  • Ticket prices. Their live site shows two different prices right now: the homepage text says Adults $20 and Seniors and Military $15, while the admission graphic on the tickets page says $15 and $12. The rebuild matches the homepage text ($20 / $15 / $15, kids 12 and under free, pit pass $40), since that is what their site states most prominently. Confirm the correct numbers with the owner. Switching them later is a 10-second edit, that is the whole point of having prices in text.
  • June 27 make-up date. That race was rained out. The schedule marks it rained out and points to Facebook for the new date. Drop in the date once it is set.
  • Race photos. The gallery and event cards use the track's own published race photos. A few carry a photographer's signature. Confirm the credit or permission before publishing.
  • Map location. The business listing uses approximate coordinates for the Moberly area. Confirm the exact spot if you want the map pin perfect.
  • Email signup. It is a working form on the demo but is not yet connected to anything. Wire it to a real provider, such as Mailchimp, to actually collect addresses.

What is in this folder

To put the site online later, this whole folder is what gets uploaded. Everything is self-contained.
